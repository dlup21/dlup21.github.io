Never Forget Ride

About the Ride

The Never Forget Ride began in 2020 with a group of cycling friends who set out to commemorate the sacrifices made by first responders during the tragic events of 9/11/2001. The challenging and picturesque ride includes two challenging hill climbs and passes through a covered bridge. Each year more friends joined us. We wanted to share this great route, help support the Tunnel to Towers Foundation and establishing this ride as an annual event!

The original ride passed by the Woodstone Country Club. Woodstone, too, has a soft spot for 9/11 first responders—they proudly display a statue named, “Lunch Atop a Skyscraper,” made from the steel recovered from the twin towers. What a perfect spot to start and finish the ride!

Original Never Forget Ride

42.5 mile ride over Blue Mountain, through covered bridge to Kunkletown and over Chestnut Ridge to follow the creek to WindGap and return to Woodstone on a scenic quiet ride along the south side of the mountain.

Short Never Forget Ride

A scenic ride through Lehigh/Moore and Bushkill Township on the south side of the Blue Mountain. Start out on a friendly, relatively flat ride and finish with a couple of sublte climbs to challenge the thighs to reflect the climb of the towers.
